Master of Technology (M.Tech)

Indian Institute of Technology Ropar

About Thesis:

This study investigated indoor air pollution at IIT Ropar and evaluated indoor plants as a mitigation strategy, monitoring PM10, PM2.5, PM1, and black carbon in classrooms, hostels, and offices using real-time instruments; results showed PM2.5 (59.6 μg/m³) and PM10 (71.12 μg/m³) levels exceeding CPCB limits, indicating significant exposure risks.

The Ajwain plant (Trachyspermum ammi) and Money plant (Epipremnum aureum) were tested for particulate reduction, with Ajwain reducing PM2.5 by 39.3% and Money plant reducing PM10 by 38.7%, leading to a practical recommendation of 20 Ajwain or 30 Money plants per room to meet CPCB standards. A health assessment using spirometry on 303 students revealed 35.97% with restrictive lung disease, 34.65% with obstructive patterns, and 74.59% asthma prevalence, demonstrating a strong link between indoor particulate exposure and respiratory health issues. This first-of-its-kind systematic study at IIT Ropar integrates air quality monitoring, plant-based intervention, and health outcomes, offering a low-cost, eco-friendly solution to improve indoor air quality in educational, office, and residential settings.
