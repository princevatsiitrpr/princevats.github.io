About

Understanding Air Pollution Dynamics Through Aerosol Research

I am a PhD researcher in Environmental Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar, working at the intersection of aerosol science, air quality monitoring, and intelligent environmental systems.

My research focuses on understanding and predicting air pollution dynamics through spatio-temporal modeling, low-cost IoT sensing networks, and data-driven environmental analytics. I develop machine learning and deep learning models—including CNNs, GNNs, and LSTMs—for PM₂.₅ prediction, air quality forecasting, and high-resolution pollution mapping by integrating atmospheric measurements with meteorological data.

My work aims to transform complex environmental data into actionable insights that support scientific discovery, public health, and evidence-based environmental policy.

    Research Domains

    Core Research Focus Areas

    🌫️ Aerosol Science

    Understanding aerosol formation, transport, and transformation in atmospheric environments, with emphasis on particle number size distribution (PNSD) reconstruction from optical/light-scattering measurements, and implications for air quality, climate interactions, and human health exposure.

    💨 Air Quality Monitoring & IoT Sensor Networks

    Real-time ambient and indoor air pollution assessment using distributed low-cost IoT sensor networks for multi-pollutant monitoring (PM2.5, PM10, CO2, CO, TVOCs), including sensor design, deployment, calibration, data fusion, quality assurance, and health risk/exposure analysis.

    🤖 AI/ML for AQ Prediction

    Development of advanced AI/ML models (CNN, GNN, LSTM, hybrid deep learning architectures) for PM₂.₅ forecasting and spatio-temporal air quality prediction, integrating meteorological, atmospheric, and sensor-network data for pattern recognition, feature extraction, and uncertainty-aware forecasting.
